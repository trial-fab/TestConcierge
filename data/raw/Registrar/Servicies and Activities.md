## Services & Activities

[Academic Advocacy](https://www.usf.edu/undergrad/academic-advocacy/index.aspx "Academic Advocacy")

The Office of Academic Advocacy (OAA) is dedicated to serving undergraduate students
in their pursuit of timely progression to graduation. Advocates individually with
students in academic distress to review their options, and collaborate with academic
departments, colleges and university personnel to improve retention and progression
to graduation.

[Academic Success Center](https://www.usf.edu/undergrad/academic-success-center/index.aspx "Academic Success Center")

The Academic Success Center (ASC) provides tutoring, writing assistance, and a SMART
Lab designed to provide hands-on learning for STEM related coursework.

[Office of Transfer Student Success](https://www.usf.edu/undergrad/transfer-student-success/index.aspx "Office of Transfer Student Success")

Advisors meet with students from community and state colleges to advise students on
USF undergraduate programs, explain program requirements, policies, procedures, transfer
application, and more.

[Office of Veteran's Success](https://www.usf.edu/student-affairs/veterans/index.aspx)

Provides specialized programs and services to veterans, eligible dependents, active
duty service members and the Selected Reserves on campus. The office helps veterans
in whatever capacity necessary to achieve their academic and career goals.

[Orientation](https://www.usf.edu/orientation/ "Orientation")

Provides every incoming student the resources to be successful, excellent customer
service and knowledge about the University of South Florida. Programing has been developed
for first year students, transfer students, international students and their families.

[Student Athlete Enrichment](https://gousfbulls.com/sports/2008/6/12/1480022.aspx "Student Athlete Enrichment")

The Student-Athlete Enrichment Center (SAEC) provides services, programming, and guidance
that is student-athlete centered and fosters academic success.

[Office of High-Impact Practices & Undergraduate Research](https://www.usf.edu/undergrad/undergraduate-research/ "HIPUR")

High-impact practices (HIPs) are active learning practices that encourage deep learning
by promoting student engagement. The Office of High Impact Practices and Undergraduate
Research (HIPUR) seeks to connect students with high-impact practices and support
faculty wishing to expand the use of HIPs in their courses.

[Air Force](https://www.usf.edu/undergrad/air-force-rotc/index.aspx "Air Force"), [Army](https://www.usf.edu/undergrad/army-rotc/ "Army ROTC"), and [Navy ROTC](https://www.usf.edu/undergrad/naval-rotc/index.aspx "Navy ROTC")

These three military divisions have Reserve Officer Training Corps (ROTC) programs
at USF which gives students the opportunity to become commissioned officers upon graduation.
