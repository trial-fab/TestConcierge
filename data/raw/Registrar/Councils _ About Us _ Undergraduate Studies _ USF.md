## Councils and Committees

[Academic Regulations Committee](https://www.usf.edu/undergrad/students/academic-regulations-committee/)
The Academic Regulations Committee (ARC) petition process occurs at the College level.
Students using this process should contact and submit the relevant documentation to
the Academic Regulations Committee (ARC) representative in the college of their declared
major. It is the student's responsibility to obtain, complete and submit all documentation
required by this process.

> [Council on Academic Advising](https://www.usf.edu/undergrad/caa/index.aspx)
> The Council on Academic Advising (CAA) is a University of South Florida body that
> provides coordinated leadership, guidance, and representation for the University's
> undergraduate academic advising community from all three campuses.

[General Education Council](https://www.usf.edu/undergrad/general-education-council/index.aspx)
The General Education Council is a standing council of the [USF Faculty Senate](https://www.usf.edu/faculty-senate/) responsible for the ongoing development, implementation, and assessment of an effective
general education program.

**Transfer Success Council**

This is an umbrella group for all things transfer as OneUSF. The charge for this group
is to champion and coordinate efforts to ensure transfer students success succeed
and graduate on time; advance transfer student success through collaboration and action
with our institutional,  community, and state partners to ensure we are transfer ready;
and promote an institutional culture that values the contributions of transfer students
and incorporates them into a culture of care that supports their success.

[Undergraduate Council](https://www.usf.edu/undergrad/undergraduate-council/index.aspx)
The Undergraduate Council is a standing council of the [USF Faculty Senate](https://www.usf.edu/faculty-senate/) responsible for recommending to the President, Provost, and the Dean of Undergraduate
Studies and reporting to the Faculty Senate on matters pertaining to undergraduate
courses, curricula, institutional programs, and degrees of the University.
