## Ethics, Conduct, and Legal Resources

At the University of South Florida (USF), students are expected to uphold values of
integrity, accountability, and respect within both academic and community settings.
This page offers direct access to support resources for students facing conduct-related
concerns, legal challenges, or personal crises. Whether you're seeking information
on academic integrity policies, how the student conduct process works, or where to
find confidential advocacy or legal support, this page serves as your central guide
to staying informed and supported at USF.

### The Student Conduct and Ethical Development

The Student Conduct and Ethical Development board assists in matters with potential
violations of Student Code of Conduct policies.

[The Student Conduct and Ethical Development](https://www.usf.edu/student-affairs/student-conduct-ethical-development/index.aspx)

---

### The Ombuds Office

The Ombuds Office helps students discuss problems, concerns, or disputes that can
be mediated informally through a third-party. The office can help students explore
solutions with their personal matters pertaining to USF or its personnel.

[The Ombuds Office](https://www.usf.edu/student-affairs/ombuds/index.aspx)

---

### EthicsPoint

EthicsPoint is a thrid-party hotline for safe and anonymous reporting potential ethics,
conduct, or abuse of USF of university systems or personnel in a non-student capacity.

[EthicsPoint](https://secure.ethicspoint.com/domain/media/en/gui/14773/index.html?locationid=-1)

---

### Legal Aid

Legal Aid provides free legal advice to students attending the University of South
Florida. Legal Aid strives to enhance public awareness by providing legal publications
and programs free of charge. The program offers free consultations to students from
Tampa Bay area attorneys.

[LegalAid](https://www.usf.edu/student-affairs/student-government/tampa-sg/tampa-services-involvement/tampa-legal-aid.aspx)
