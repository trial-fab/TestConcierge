#### Details: Graduate Orientation

[Show What is the difference between this and my college orientation?](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/orientation-faq.aspx)

Graduate Studies' Orientation **introduces you to USF**, whereas your college orientation introduces you to your college information.

The Office of Graduate Studies provides an orientation experience for students across
all colleges. **Take the opportunity to meet students while attending various social, professional
development, and academic-based sessions.**

Should your program offer their own orientation, please inquire directly with the
major contact here: <https://usf.box.com/s/r3lodc6ah0wkszzi409pz1r4p4rtrhfy>

[Show Canvas course vs. In-Person Orientation?](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/orientation-faq.aspx)

The Canvas course was developed by OGS for all graduate students, it is a resource
that will remain in your Canvas account for at least your first semester. *You are automatically placed into the group, and will be automatically removed.*

The Canvas course is a hub of information that is important to all graduate students,
regardless of their program! *Revisit the course for helpful links and videos about Financial Aid, Health and Wellness
Services, Student Account Services, and Graduate Studies information.*

[üîó Visit Canvas](https://usflearn.instructure.com/)

The in-person orientation, hosted by OGS, is a single-day event comprised of interactive
and engaging opportunities. There will be various interest sessions, an organization
fair, and opportunities to meet other graduate students! Offerings may vary by year.

[üîó View In-Person Orientation Information](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/new-graduate-student-orientation.aspx)

#### Admissions

[Show How can I have my residency status adjusted/re-evaluated?](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/orientation-faq.aspx)

To have your residency status readjusted to that of a Florida resident, you will need
to submit the form at the following link to admissions@usf.edu as soon as possible but before the first day of classes: <https://www.usf.edu/admissions/documents/gr-florida-residency-for-tuition.pdf>

[Show How do I know if I qualify for Florida Residency?](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/orientation-faq.aspx)

The State of Florida requires submitting two documents, which must be dated/issued
12 months before the first day of classes for the semester for which Florida residency
is sought.

You can find a complete listing of the required documents and an outline of our residency
guidelines here: <https://www.usf.edu/admissions/graduate/admission-information/residency-information.aspx>

[Show How do I defer my entry to a later semester?](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/orientation-faq.aspx)

To have your acceptance deferred to a later term, please email a request to defer
with your U-ID# and the specific term to which you would like to defer to your respective
Graduate Director. See here for contact info: <https://usf.app.box.com/s/r3lodc6ah0wkszzi409pz1r4p4rtrhfy>

[Show How to I submit official copies of my transcripts/test scores?](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/orientation-faq.aspx)

Within 30 days of the beginning of the semester, submit an official transcript from
each institution you have attended and official test scores sent directly from the

#### Registration

[Show How do I go about registering for classes?](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/orientation-faq.aspx)

Class registration is completed through MyUSF, **Student Self-Service**. (Access Student Self-Service by logging into MyUSF)

**Detailed overview of the course registration process: <https://www.usf.edu/registrar/register/>**

[Show How do I get a course permit?](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/orientation-faq.aspx)

Some courses or sections of a course, have restrictions such as pre-requisites, co-requisites
or permission by academic department that are required in order to register.

Your major‚Äôs designated Graduate Director is a great person to start with to secure
a permit, but you can also find additional departmental contact information here:
<https://www.usf.edu/registrar/services/permit.aspx>

[Show When is the last day to register for classes?](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/orientation-faq.aspx)

Refer to the USF Registrar's calendar: <https://www.usf.edu/registrar/calendars/>

The last day to register is typically the Friday before the start of classes, without
incurring a late registration fee, but you can continue to add/drop classes through
your Student Self-Service account throughout the first week of classes until the next
Friday. **Please check the official university [registrar calendar](https://www.usf.edu/registrar/calendars/%20%20%20) to be sure.**

Beyond that point, you‚Äôll need to connect with your designated graduate director for
your major to be added late via a graduate petition.

[Show When do classes begin this semester?](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/orientation-faq.aspx)

Please refer to the USF Registrar's calendar: <https://www.usf.edu/registrar/calendars/>

[Show How do I register for classes if I am attending USF on a state employee tuition waiver?](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/orientation-faq.aspx)

You can find a detailed overview of the state employee tuition waiver process at the
link embedded below, but the most important thing to remember is not to register until
the Friday prior to the start of classes otherwise you will be automatically deemed
ineligible to receive the waiver: <https://www.usf.edu/business-finance/controller/student-services/student-accounting/waivers-state.aspx>

[Show NET ID vs. USF ID](https://www.usf.edu/graduate-studies/students/newly-admitted-students/orientation/orientation-faq.aspx)

Your USFID is your personal student identification number and is printed below your
photo on your USFID card. USFID numbers are originally given to you via email in your
acceptance letter. It is also referred to as a U# and looks similar to U12345678.

*This information is often used in academic processes.*

Your **NetID** is your user ID and allows access to a variety of online services offered by the
university including your email, Student Self-Service (formerly OASIS) account, and
Canvas. It looks similar to a username (e.g. ‚Äòjdoe‚Äô) and will be the beginning part
of your USF email (e.g. ‚Äòjdoe@mail.usf.edu‚Äô).
*Daily use, this is the ID associated with the Microsoft software.*
